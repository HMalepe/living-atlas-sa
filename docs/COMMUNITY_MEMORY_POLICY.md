# Community Memory Policy — Living Atlas SA

## Purpose

Community memory is valuable civic knowledge — but it is not the same as verified official history.

## What users may submit

- Oral histories and voice recordings
- Old photographs (with consent)
- Nicknames and alternative name explanations
- Family recollections
- Former business addresses
- Historical documents
- Pronunciations
- Cultural sky stories

## Consent and attribution

- Explicit contributor consent checkbox
- Attribution choices: full name, initials, anonymous
- Contact information stored separately from public display
- Right to request removal

## Moderation workflow

```
Submitted
  → Automated safety and duplication checks
  → Source and consent review
  → Geographic review (is this about the claimed place?)
  → Editorial review
  → Community-context review
  → Published | Disputed | Rejected | Archived
```

## Display rules

| Submission type | Display label |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Oral history | "Oral history" |
| Family recollection | "Community memory" |
| Photograph | "Community photograph" + date if known |
| Disputed account | "Disputed account" with link to other claims |

**Never** convert "my grandfather said" into an official fact.

## Cultural and indigenous knowledge

- May require community ownership metadata
- `permission_status`: pending | granted | restricted | revoked
- `display_restrictions`: public | members_only | research_only
- `commercial_reuse`: allowed | restricted | prohibited
- `community_owner_org_id` for organisational stewardship

## Safety

- No encouragement of unsafe roadside stopping
- Location of submissions approximate by default
- Strip EXIF from uploaded images
- Rate limits on public submissions
- Reporting and moderation tools

## AI usage

AI may transcribe or translate drafts. AI may not publish community submissions automatically or resolve cultural ownership.
