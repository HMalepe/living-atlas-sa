# Product Vision — Living Atlas SA

## Promise

> **Point anywhere. Understand what you are seeing, why it exists, what it was called, how it connects, and what people believed about it.**

## What this is

Living Atlas SA is not a conventional educational website, municipal database, static map, generic tourism app, or AI-generated encyclopedia.

It must feel like:

- A playable map of reality
- A pointable encyclopedia
- A civic memory engine
- A road-network anatomy tool
- A Southern Hemisphere planetarium
- A geographical discovery game
- A research platform
- A national cultural archive
- A premium African technology product

## Core loop

**Point → Identify → Explore → Connect → Understand → Remember → Return**

## Dual knowledge systems

1. **Ground** — significant South African roads and their connected environments (first module)
2. **Sky** — the sky as observed from South Africa

Future modules: buildings, neighbourhoods, rivers, mountains, railways, geology, wildlife, languages, infrastructure, historical events, architecture, cultural landscapes.

## Module tree

```
Living Atlas SA
├── Ground (Roads, Intersections, Places, Infrastructure)
├── Sky (Moon, Sun, Planets, Stars, Constellations, Deep Sky)
├── Time (Historical Maps, Naming Changes, Construction Phases, Past/Future Skies)
├── Memory (Official Records, Community Recollections, Oral Histories, Disputed Accounts)
└── Journeys (Road Expeditions, Night-Sky Trails, Heritage Routes, Learning Quests)
```

## Design principles

- Premium, exploratory, cinematic, intelligent, African, contemporary, calm
- No clichéd safari imagery, tribal patterns, loud flag colours, or generic "Africa tech" stereotypes
- Evidence over assertion; community memory shown as memory
- Mobile-first exploration; desktop-first research
- Extensible architecture — roads are the first Ground module, not the permanent centre of gravity

## Standard

> One of the most thoughtful, beautiful, trustworthy, and technically ambitious exploration products built anywhere, rooted unapologetically in South Africa.
